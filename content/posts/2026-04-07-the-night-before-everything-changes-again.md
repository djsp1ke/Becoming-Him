# The night before everything changes. Again.

*It's 11pm. Tomorrow I start 75 Hard. Here's what's going through my head.*

**James Gaunt · 8 April 2026 · 5 min read**

---

I'm sitting here, it's late, and tomorrow I start 75 Hard again. I've done this before. I know what it feels like on Day 1 — that clean, buzzing, almost-manic clarity you get when you've made a decision you actually mean. I want to bottle that feeling. I also know what it feels like on Day 37 when you're lying on the couch at 10:30pm and you still haven't taken your progress photo. I know, because that's where my last attempt ended.

A photo. After 37 days of getting up early, two workouts a day, 3.8 litres of water, at least 10 pages every single night — I didn't take a photo. I know how stupid that sounds. I know. But that's the point of 75 Hard, isn't it? It's not the hard things that break you. It's the thing you think doesn't matter.

> *"It wasn't the workouts that beat me. It was the complacency."*

So tonight I'm not pretending I've got this sorted. I'm not writing some motivational post about how this time is different because I've found my "why" or I've got a new routine. I'm just being honest with you: I'm nervous. I'm 45 years old, I turned a year older last Friday, and I still have days where I don't like the person in the mirror. Not in a dramatic way. Just — there's a gap between who I am and who I know I can be. 75 Hard is me deciding to close that gap by 75 days' worth.

People who've followed me for a while know the road I've been on. Gambling from the age of 9. Alcohol. Cocaine. Weed. Things I'm not proud of, but things I've stopped hiding from because hiding them was costing me more than telling the truth ever could. I've been sober from alcohol for 2 years before I can do it again. Not drinking in 2026. And the version of me writing this tonight is one I genuinely like better than most versions that came before.

But liking who you are and becoming who you're meant to be — those are two different things. And I'm still becoming him.

> *"I was a Slimming World Man of the Year finalist. Then I lost my way. I'm finding it again."*

Tomorrow morning I start Day 1 of 75 Hard. The rules are simple and brutal: two 45-minute workouts — one has to be outside, no excuses — 3.8 litres of water, 10 pages of non-fiction, follow the Slimming World plan, and take a progress photo every single day. If I miss anything, even the photo, I go back to Day 1. That's the deal.

My finish date is 22 June 2026. I'm writing that down so it's real. I'm sharing it so you can hold me to it. The whole point of this account, this person, this **Becoming Him** thing — it's not about me looking good. It's about me showing you that doing hard things is how you build a life you actually want to live.

I'm going to document all of it. The good mornings and the rough ones. The days when I feel unstoppable and the days when I'm sitting in the car park of a gym at 6am asking myself why I'm doing this. I'll film it. I'll post it. I won't clean it up.

One thing I know for certain: the man I want to become isn't waiting for conditions to be perfect. He just does it. Tomorrow, I start.

See you everyday for the next 75 days.

*— James*
